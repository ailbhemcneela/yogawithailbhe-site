I came to yoga for the movement. I stayed for everything else.

Fifteen years of practice, a long career in corporate, two children, and a lot of life in between.

I've been practising yoga for fifteen years. When I first found it, I was drawn in by what it could do for my body — the strength, the stretch, the way it felt to move through a sequence. I used it as a workout. I used it for a good stretch. For a long time, that was enough.

But over the years, through everything that life brings — a career of over fifteen years in corporate environments, working my way up to director level, becoming a mother twice over, illness, and all the changes that come with a life fully lived — I noticed it was never the poses that held me together. It was the breathing. The stillness. The philosophy. The way an hour on the mat could return me to myself when everything else had taken me away from it.

Yoga has always been there. Through the difficult periods, the transitions, the moments when I didn't quite know who I was outside of my job or my role as a mother. It helped me understand something I really needed to understand: that I am many things at once. There is space for all of it.

"Yoga has made me realise that I don't have to just be the corporate Ailbhe, or the yoga teacher Ailbhe, or any single version of myself. I am many things — and there is space for all of them."

I kept noticing the same pattern in myself and in the people around me. We know we need to slow down. We know we should take better care of ourselves. And yet somehow we always end up at the bottom of our own list. I wanted to create something that made it easier to choose yourself — even just for an hour. Something you could look forward to. Something that felt like a ritual rather than another obligation.

In early 2025, after eight months of teacher training, I qualified as a yoga teacher. I had been a student for fifteen years before that — which meant I came to teaching with a very clear picture of the kind of space I wanted to create, and the kind I didn't.

My classes are small by design. Never more than ten people. I want you to feel seen. I want you to leave feeling like yourself again — not like you just survived something.

If you're someone who knows they need to slow down but hasn't quite given themselves permission yet — this is for you.
